Kai Stephens

I build software, local AI systems, and infrastructure, usually around tools that make technical work faster, more private, or easier to run yourself. At 15, I climbed Kilimanjaro, and that same kind of long-term obsession shows up in how I work: hard projects, patient systems, and ideas that take more than a weekend to become useful.

I started Nipux to work on private AI infrastructure and self-hosted systems, and I worked at Treefrog Inc. as a software developer. My projects tend to sit between product and engineering: automation, robotics, study software, dashboards, local model workflows, and the backend pieces that keep them reliable.

I open-source models on Hugging Face that have surpassed half a million downloads, and I share experiments with 20k+ people on X. I care about practical AI, local compute, clear interfaces, and building things people can actually run, inspect, and improve.
